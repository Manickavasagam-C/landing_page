The Access Problem

Space science shouldn't require a sovereign budget.

For decades, reaching orbit meant billions of dollars, years of bureaucracy, and access reserved for national space agencies and aerospace giants. If your research needed orbital data, you waited. If your university wanted real satellite experience, you built simulations.

The result: Invaluable research stays grounded. IoT networks that need space-based coverage can't afford it. Indian engineers build the world's satellites — and launch them for others.

That gap ends here.

Cost Barrier Traditional orbital deployment runs $50M–$500M. Only governments and aerospace primes can play.
Complexity Barrier Launch logistics, ground station infrastructure, orbital mechanics — the operational burden stops good science before it starts.
Access Barrier India's next generation of space engineers train on textbooks — while other nations send students to orbit.

The Mission Sequence

From blueprint to orbit in five stages.

Every ToSpace mission follows the same verified sequence — the same path IndianSat took, the same path your satellite will take.

01
Stage 1 — Research & Design

Mission architecture

We begin with your payload requirements. Our engineering team designs the satellite structure, sensor integration, and communication architecture — optimized for your scientific or commercial objective.

02
Stage 2 — Prototype & Ground Testing

Built and tested on Earth first

Using 3D-printed graphene polymer frames and integrated PCB systems, we build and stress-test your satellite under simulated orbital conditions — thermal cycling, vibration, vacuum chamber validation.

03
Stage 3 — HAB Near-Space Validation

Stratospheric verification at 37km

Before any orbital commitment, your payload ascends on a High-Altitude Balloon to 18–37km — experiencing -40°C temperatures and near-vacuum pressure. Data validated here is data trusted in orbit.

04
Stage 4 — Ground Station Integration

Your data pipeline, ready before launch

We configure your ground station for telemetry reception — antenna pointing, downlink protocols, and data processing pipelines — so you're ready to receive the moment your satellite passes overhead.

05
Stage 5 — Launch & Live Data

Orbit achieved. Mission active.

Your satellite deploys from our SpaceX launch aggregation slot. Within hours, your first telemetry arrives. You're no longer watching space — you're operating in it.

STEM from the Stratosphere

We don't teach space science. We do it with students.

In Sirkali, Tamil Nadu, 50+ school students built PocketQube satellites in two days — and sent them to the stratosphere using a High-Altitude Balloon. At 37km altitude, their satellites measured pressure, temperature, and signal strength in near-vacuum conditions.

This is not a classroom exercise. These are real measurements from real instruments at real altitude.

Mission Briefing

Everything you need to know.

Direct answers to the questions institutional clients and educators ask before partnering with ToSpace.

Is ToSpace technically capable of delivering a functional orbital satellite? +

Yes — with verifiable proof. We are the first Indian startup with satellites scheduled for launch via SpaceX. IndianSat has been validated through ground testing and stratospheric HAB missions reaching 37km altitude, operating at -40°C in near-vacuum conditions. We are supported by Tamil Nadu Technology Hub (iTNT) and featured at Indian Mobile Congress and GIM 2024.

What exactly is a PocketQube satellite? Is it capable of real scientific work? +

A PocketQube is a standardized satellite format measured in units of 5×5×5cm (1P). ToSpace's IndianSat carries 11 sensors monitoring 20+ parameters including temperature, pressure, and radiation. Our 1P–6P and 1U–6U configurations accommodate single-sensor technology demonstrations all the way to multi-payload research missions.

What is the end-to-end process if my institution wants to deploy a satellite? +

Consultation and mission scoping → payload design and specification → prototype manufacturing and ground testing → stratospheric validation via HAB → ground station setup → launch slot assignment via our SpaceX aggregation → orbital deployment and live telemetry. Timeline varies by mission complexity; contact us to discuss your specific requirements.

How much does a PocketQube satellite mission cost? +

Mission costs vary based on configuration (1P vs. 6U), payload complexity, ground station requirements, and launch timeline. ToSpace is designed for institutional affordability — our approach to aggregated launches fundamentally reduces costs compared to independent launch contracting. Contact us for a mission scoping call and a structured estimate.

Can you build a satellite for our specific research payload or IoT use case? +

Yes. All ToSpace satellites are designed around your payload requirements, not vice versa. We have developed configurations for IoT/LoRa communication (IndianSat V2), environmental sensing, and multi-spectral imaging. We design the mission architecture to match your science or commercial objective.

How do HAB missions work and can our students participate? +

High-Altitude Balloon missions lift payloads to 18–37km altitude where temperature drops to -40°C and pressure approaches near-vacuum. Students build the PocketQube satellite in our workshop, integrate it as the HAB payload, and receive live telemetry during ascent. We run these missions across Tamil Nadu and can bring the programme to your school or university.

You are a bootstrapped startup — what gives us confidence in long-term reliability? +

Being bootstrapped means every technical decision is driven by engineering merit, not investor timeline pressure. Our milestones — SpaceX launch partnership, iTNT support, national recognition — were earned, not funded. We are in active discussions with universities and satellite companies for MOU partnerships that expand our operational capacity as we scale.
