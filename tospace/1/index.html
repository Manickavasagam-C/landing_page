What satellite configurations do you support?

ToSpace builds PocketQube satellites in 1P through 6P configurations and CubeSat-standard 1U through 6U formats. Our flagship IndianSat is a 1P (3×3×3 cm) form factor. We support custom payload integration, sensor suites, and communication payloads including LoRa RF for IoT applications.

How does a HAB launch work, and what does it cost vs. orbital launch?

HAB (High-Altitude Balloon) missions reach 18–37 km altitude — the stratosphere — where conditions approach space: −40°C temperatures, near-vacuum pressure, and intense UV exposure. HAB launches cost a fraction of orbital deployment and are ideal for validating payloads before committing to a full launch. Contact us for mission-specific pricing.

Is IndianSat truly the world's smallest satellite?

IndianSat weighs 30 grams and measures just 3×3×3 cm — making it among the lightest and smallest functional satellites ever built. It is fully 3D-printed from reinforced graphene polymer, integrates an onboard computer with 11 sensors, and measures 20+ environmental parameters. IndianSat V2 adds LoRa-based IoT communication from orbit.

How can my university or school partner with ToSpace for a workshop?

We're actively expanding our STEM programme across Tamil Nadu and beyond. Our workshop format has students building PocketQube satellites and deploying them via HAB launch — all within 48 hours. We're also in active discussions with universities to embed space technology into engineering and science curricula. Reach out via our contact section to explore collaboration.

Which launch vehicle carries ToSpace satellites to orbit?

ToSpace is the first Indian startup with satellites scheduled for launch aboard SpaceX vehicles. We provide aggregated launch services for nano satellite customers, meaning we coordinate launch slots across missions to keep deployment costs accessible for Indian research institutions, universities, and commercial operators.
