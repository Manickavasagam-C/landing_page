The Full Mission Arc

Every Satellite We Build Goes the Full Distance.

We don't hand you a kit and wish you luck. ToSpace runs the complete mission — from the first circuit design in our lab to the moment your data comes back from orbit.

Stage 01

Design & Engineering

Every ToSpace satellite begins in our Karur R&D lab. We integrate your payload with an on-board computer running 11 sensors capable of measuring 20+ parameters. Structure is reinforced graphene polymer — 3D printed for mass and precision tolerances that orbital launch demands.

Stage 02

Near-Space Validation

Before anything reaches orbit, it earns it. We deploy every satellite payload aboard High-Altitude Balloon missions to 18–37km — into true stratospheric conditions: pressure near zero, temperatures at -40°C. Hardware that survives HAB testing is hardware that's ready for space.

Stage 03

Launch & Deployment

ToSpace is the first Indian startup with satellites scheduled for SpaceX launch — providing Indian researchers, companies, and institutions direct access to orbital deployment at PocketQube scale. We manage the entire launch aggregation, documentation, and deployment process.

Stage 04

Mission Data & Control

Orbit isn't the end — it's when the work begins. Our ground station support and LoRa IoT communication architecture brings your satellite data home. IndianSat V2 provides open-source, secure IoT connectivity across LoRa gateways. Your mission, measured.

Common Questions

Everything You Need to Know Before You Reach Out.

Is ToSpace capable of actually getting my payload into orbit — or is this aspirational?

ToSpace is the first Indian startup with satellites formally scheduled for SpaceX launch. That's not aspirational — it's documented. Our milestones include recognition from iTNT Hub, representation at Tamil Nadu GIM 2024, and hardware that's already been to the stratosphere via HAB missions. We're a bootstrapped startup, which means every milestone we claim has been earned without the cushion of external funding.

What payload types and sizes do you support?

We support PocketQube configurations from 1P to 6P and CubeSat-compatible 1U to 6U form factors. IoT sensors, communication modules, imaging hardware, and environmental monitoring payloads have all been explored in our development work. Contact us with your specifications and we'll assess integration feasibility within 5 business days.

How does the HAB testing program work, and is it required?

HAB testing is strongly recommended but not mandatory. For educational missions, it's typically the primary deliverable — students build the satellite, we launch it to 37km, and it returns data to ground. For commercial payloads destined for orbit, HAB testing provides stratospheric validation before committing to launch costs. A HAB mission costs significantly less than orbital launch and catches design issues early.

What does a satellite mission with ToSpace actually cost?

Pricing depends on configuration, payload complexity, and mission timeline. PocketQube satellite builds and HAB missions are priced for accessibility — our entire mission is making space affordable for Indian organizations. HAB educational missions are the most accessible entry point. Contact us for a scoped estimate — we respond within 48 hours.

Can you work with universities and engineering colleges?

Yes — and we're actively seeking MOU partnerships with universities. Our STEM program is already in talks with several institutions to integrate PocketQube satellite building into engineering and physics curricula. University partnerships can access our hardware, R&D support, and HAB launch infrastructure as part of structured academic programs.

How long does a full satellite mission take from brief to launch?

A HAB educational mission typically takes 4–8 weeks from brief to launch day. A custom orbital satellite build follows a longer timeline depending on payload complexity, launch window availability, and testing iterations. We discuss realistic timelines in the initial scoping conversation — we won't set a timeline we can't deliver.

What happens to my satellite after orbital deployment?

Post-deployment, your satellite operates in LEO with ground station support provided by ToSpace. For IndianSat V2-based missions, LoRa communication brings mission data back to ground via our gateway infrastructure. We support mission operations for the satellite's operational lifetime and provide data reporting for research and publication purposes.
