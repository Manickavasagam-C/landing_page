Frequently Asked

What satellite form factors and configurations do you support?
ToSpace designs and builds PocketQube satellites in 1P through 6P form factors and CubeSat-standard 1U through 6U configurations. IndianSat is a 1P (3×3×3 cm) form factor. We support custom payload integration across IoT, imaging, atmospheric sensing, and RF communication missions.
How does a HAB launch differ from orbital deployment — and what does it cost?
HAB (High-Altitude Balloon) missions take payloads to 18–37 km altitude where conditions closely approximate near-space: temperatures as low as −40°C, near-vacuum pressures, and intense UV exposure. HAB launches cost a small fraction of orbital deployment and are designed as a validation step before committing to a full launch. Contact us for mission-specific pricing.
How does IndianSat's LoRa IoT communication work in orbit?
IndianSat V2 carries a LoRa (Long Range RF) communication payload that tests low-power wide-area data transmission from orbit to LoRa gateway networks on the ground. LoRa's chirp-spread-spectrum modulation allows communication over hundreds of kilometres with minimal power — making it ideal for IoT sensor relay from space. The platform is open-source.
Which launch vehicle carries ToSpace satellites, and how is the rideshare structured?
ToSpace is the first Indian startup with satellites scheduled for launch aboard SpaceX vehicles. We operate as a PocketQube launch aggregator — consolidating multiple small payloads into shared launch slots to minimise per-satellite launch costs. This aggregated model makes orbital access economically viable for Indian research institutions and commercial operators.
How can our university or school partner with ToSpace?
We're actively expanding our STEM programme across Tamil Nadu. Our 48-hour workshop format has students designing and building PocketQube satellites and deploying them via HAB launch — returning real stratospheric telemetry. We're also in active discussions with universities to embed space technology into engineering and science curricula via formal MOU. Reach out via the contact section below.
